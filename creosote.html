COMPRESS — Eight Compressors, True Circuits

Not eight presets of one compressor — eight true hardware topologies.

  • · The Models — LEVEL CELL (opto) · BLACK FET (1176) · ALL BUTTONS · FAIR MU (vari-mu) · SSL BUS · NEVE BRIDGE · PYE PWM · TG ZENER.
  • · True Topology — Opto, vari-mu, bridge, and PWM units run feedback. FET, VCA, and Zener units run feedforward. Force either topology in TWEAK.

SUMMING & STAGING — The Console Bus

Character saturation, stereo glue, and a ceiling clipper — all anti-derivative anti-aliased (ADAA), all measured.

  • · Summing — PREGAIN, WIDTH (M/S), BALANCE. The last analog bus before the wide world.
  • · IRON — A modeled output transformer (off / subtle / rich) with three voicings (British console, American punch, broadcast desk) built as a flux integrator with magnetic remanence, not an EQ curve.
  • · Staging — Honest INPUT/LEVEL staging. Limit lookahead ceiling pouring oil-damped reduction top-down. AUTO LEVEL for one-button unity out.
  • · TONE — A direction-aware tilt. Darkening reaches into the presence region to *warm* rather than dull. Brightening lifts the air band and tightens lows.
OUTPUT EQ — The Chart Plate

Eight bands of dynamic EQ drawn on a machine-tool chart: cream card, blue graticule, red pen.

  • · Dynamic Architecture — Per-band frequency, gain, Q, plus dynamic gain with attack and release — each band can duck or expand itself against the signal.
  • · Visual Feedback — Pre and post curves displayed together (the dashed blueprint and the live red line) with the dynamic delta filled between them.
  • · Decramped, Analog-Matched Filters — Standard digital EQs cramp against Nyquist. CREOSOTE's shelves and bells are matched to their analog prototypes to within 0.16 dB at 20 kHz. The air band behaves like the circuit, not the sample rate.

Unit Variance

No two racks alike

Real hardware never matched. CREOSOTE models that: pick UNIT 1 through 16 and the instance adopts that unit's component tolerances — filter corners drift up to ±5%, drive points shift fractions of a dB, the channels mismatch just slightly. Deterministic, recall-safe, and loudness-matched to ±0.005 dB.

Punch Protection

Saturate the sustain

Heavy drive usually costs you your transients. PUNCH detects onsets and momentarily eases the drive into the saturators (level-compensated, wet-path only) so the crack of the stick passes clean while the body stays thick. Measured: attacks pass ~1.4 dB cleaner at FULL.

The Console
