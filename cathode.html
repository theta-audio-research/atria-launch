CATHODE

Character Processor and Creative Degradation Instrument

CATHODE treats degradation as one coherent system. Instead of generic lo-fi modules, it operates on the premise that the sound is the transmission, and the plugin is the unstable machine carrying it. At the core of CATHODE is a signal path built around heat, instability, interruption, and control. It can add tube-like weight and asymmetry, induce worn-playback movement, capture fractured glitch states, and push material into forms of failure that still feel musical.

MODULE_DETAIL

LOCK

Unstable continuity, sync stress, and relock behavior. LOCK is the part of CATHODE that makes audio feel like a signal that cannot quite hold together. It is not just movement. It is continuity under stress.

At its core, LOCK is about the behavior of a receiver trying to remain coherent while vertical hold, timing, and slip start to fail. The composite LOCK control combines: WARP, SLIP, and RECOVER.

HEAD

Wear, drift, and imperfect contact. This module is about mechanical uncertainty: worn contact, alignment drift, and mistracking. The goal was to move away from rhythmic pumping and toward irregular, physical instability.

The composite HEAD control combines: WEAR, DRIFT, and MIX.

RECEIVER

Automatic gain behavior and continuity failure. This section combines AGC compensation and dropout failure. AGC creates brightness pump via PUMP and SPEED. DROP handles broken continuity via RATE and DEPTH.

PHOSPHOR

Heat, fatigue, width, and display chemistry. This is where the plugin starts to feel aged and luminous. AGE combines phosphor fatigue (DULL) and signal LOSS. HEAT pushes the chemistry via WARM and presence BUMP.

INTERFERENCE

Bursts, static, competing stations, and takeover. Developed around the "dueling stations" concept. BURST controls incident behavior, NOISE governs the contamination bed, and COMPETE determines takeover aggressiveness.

TRANSIENT

Attack, sustain, and physical impact. A surgical shaping module used to keep broken systems punchy. ATTACK pushes the front edge, SUSTAIN defines the body length, and MIX handles the final balance.
