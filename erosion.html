EROSION

Four-Stage Saturation Engine

EROSION treats degradation as a physical process. Built around four distinct analog circuit models, it provides a flexible routing architecture paired with a performance-grade XY control surface. Instead of offering disconnected modules, EROSION treats saturation as a coherent signal environment. The four-voice engine covers the full spectrum of nonlinear transformation—from invisible germanium warmth to aggressive HG-2 tube destruction. Character is anchored by the GRIME XY pad—a functional surface that simultaneously sets tube character (Pentode, Triode, 12AT7) and drives performance effects like Grain Scatter and Stutter Gate. Everything is finished in a master output section with soft-clipping transients and a brick-wall limiter. EROSION does not just color sound; it steers it through a signal path built for technical beauty and controlled failure.

ANALOG_MODELS

  • DISTRESS [GERMANIUM] Transistor compressor-saturator with asymmetric clipping and unpredictable harmonic crunch.
  • AMBER [TAPE] Phoenix-style tape saturation with frequency-dependent tilt weighting.
  • GRIME [TUBE] Three-path HG-2 simulation (Pentode, Triode, 12AT7) driven by an XY heat pad.
  • GRIT [TANH] Symmetrical soft-clipping waveshaper for clean edge and presence.

XY_PERFORMANCE

  • Grain Scatter Time-scattered micro-grains with subtle pitch randomization and swarm density control.
  • Stutter Gate Physical drag-velocity rhythm. Signal chops into patterns based on cursor movement.
  • Feedback Smear Resonant comb filtering with self-oscillating feedback feeding into the tube stages.

SIGNAL_ROUTING

  • Series Sequential chain (Distress → Amber → Grime → Grit) for compound character development.
  • A/B Split Parallel paths (Distress/Amber vs Grime/Grit) for character balancing.
  • Parallel Summing Independent level and phase control for all four modules feeding a master summation.
