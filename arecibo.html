ARECIBO

Interstellar Granular Processor

ARECIBO is a cinematic sound-transformation plugin from THETA Audio Research. At its core is a layered source engine and motion chain that moves through THETA Source Array → DRIFT → BREATHE → FIELD → FADE → PAN, then into a deeper output architecture built around pre-QUORRA tone shaping, the QUORRA motion engine, and a final Wall Stage for transient control and limiting. Where many plugins give you a list of effects, ARECIBO gives you a system. You can shape frequency before motion, push the signal into QUORRA for shuffle, warp, scan movement, and node-focused character, then control the final impact with a dedicated wall stage. QUORRA is designed as the last major transformation stage before the final limiter, which makes the plugin feel intentional: first shape, then destabilize, then force the result into a controlled boundary. The visual language follows THETA’s canonical “Void” design system: deep black backgrounds, restrained metallic accents, thin vector detail, and typography built around Montserrat and JetBrains Mono. The interface is meant to feel like a scientific transmission console rather than a conventional plugin shell. ARECIBO is especially effective on: • pads and drones • vocals and melodic material • sound design layers • FX tails and atmospheres • drums and percussion that need motion, pressure, or controlled destabilization Use it to create spectral movement, unstable harmonics, evolving texture, focused pressure, and cinematic transformation across a single continuous chain. ARECIBO does not just color sound — it steers it.

CORE_FEATURES

  • Source Array engine with layered voice architecture and sample-based source capability.
  • Motion chain built around DRIFT, BREATHE, FIELD, FADE, and PAN for spatial and temporal transformation.
  • Pre-QUORRA shaping designed to prepare the signal before deeper motion processing.
  • QUORRA transformation stage for warp, shuffle, doppler-like movement, sweep behavior, and node-focused character.
  • Wall Stage with transient shaping and final limiter behavior for controlled impact.
  • THETA Void interface language with flat rendering, thin vectors, and a scientific control-surface aesthetic.

USE_CASES

  • Turn static tones into living atmospheres
  • Add cinematic instability to pads, drones, and vocals
  • Shape sound before motion, then push it into controlled collapse
  • Create pressure, blur, drift, sweep, and impact inside one chain
