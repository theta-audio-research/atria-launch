THESEUS

Spatial Reverb Instrument

RETURN_TO_LAB STORE

THESEUS is a spatial reverb instrument built around a modern feedback-delay-network engine. The physical size of an environment and the overall length of its tail are treated as two decoupled operations. You can isolate a sound source within an intimate room running an infinite tail, or cast a vast cathedral setting that chokes short—without the two performance parameters conflicting.

The Engine

Rooms to cathedrals

A precision feedback-delay-network core that moves seamlessly from tight spaces up to infinite halls. Dense, smooth, and uncolored, eliminating the artificial metallic artifacts common in legacy reverb designs.

Independent control

Size & Decay, decoupled

Configure discrete spatial metrics independent of room ring constraints. Force tight room shapes to trail off into infinite delays, or restrict sprawling halls to tight, gated reflections without parameter interference.

Spatial mapping

Built for the field

Scalable deployment across mono, stereo, and complex immersive formats. Front, side, rear, and height fields are individually targeted as discrete decorrelated spaces, sharing unified group control arrays.

Modulation

Living tails

Slow, decorrelated internal movement tracks along reverb tails to keep long-form generation routines continuously breathing and organic, avoiding static states without tipping into obvious chorus textures.

Acoustic Tone

Shape the air

Integrates frequency-dependent decay alongside input and output equalization strips. Carve dark, dense environments or highly diffused, bright reflections with micro-variable density thresholds.

Reveal Gate

Gate the tail

Complete structural threshold, attack, hold, and release parameters driving the trailing reverb tail. Supports native MIDI re-triggering for rhythmic sculpting and precision ambient automation gating.
